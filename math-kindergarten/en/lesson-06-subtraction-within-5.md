# Lesson 6 — Subtraction Within 5

## 📋 Learning Goals
- Understand subtraction as "taking away"
- Recognize the minus sign `-`
- Subtract numbers within 5

## 🤔 Page 2: Taking Away!

Alex walked over:

> "This is **subtraction**, Steve. Subtraction means 'take away' or 'remove'. We see what's **left**."

She picked up 5 small stones:

> "I have 5 stones. Take away 2 — 1, 2. Count what's left: 1, 2, 3. **3** stones remain!"
> "Written as: **5 - 2 = 3**"

**`-`** = **minus sign**, means "take away" or "remove".

## ❌ Common Mistakes

- ❌ **Confusing subtraction with addition**
  Seeing "4 - 1" and somehow getting 5.
  ✅ **Subtraction means taking away**: 4 things, remove 1, left with **3**.

- ❌ **Only looking at the first number, ignoring what's taken**
  Example: "5 - 3" — just saying 5.
  ✅ **Start with 5, take away 3, count what's left**: 4, 3, 2. Answer is **2**.



## 🧠 Think About It

1. **Observation**: If you have 5 cookies and eat 2, how many are left? Can you use your fingers to check?

2. **What if**: In "4 - 1", what if you took away 0 instead of 1? How many would be left?



## 🔗 Cross-Curricular Links

- **Reading & Writing**: Practice full sentences: "There were 4 cookies. I ate 1. Now there are 3."
  Vocabulary: **subtraction, minus, take away, left, fewer**

- **Art**: Draw 5 stars, then cross out 2. How many are left?
