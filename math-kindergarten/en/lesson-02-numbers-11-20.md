# Lesson 2 — Numbers 11 to 20

## 📋 Learning Goals
- Recognize and write numbers 11 through 20
- Understand "groups of ten": 10 things = one "ten"
- Understand 11-20 as "some tens + some ones"

---

> [Standard: K.CC.A.1 — Count to 100 by ones and by tens]
> [Standard: K.NBT.A.1 — Compose and decompose numbers 11-19 into ten ones and further ones]

## 🎬 Page 1: Forest Adventure

Steve packed his bag and walked into a thick forest.

> "Alex, look! There are so many berries! And mushrooms! And…"

Alex laughed:

> "We've got enough food for days. But Steve… can you count past 10?"

Steve scratched his head:

> "Uh… after 10 comes… uh… both hands are out. Then I get stuck."

Alex patted his shoulder:

> "Don't worry! Let me teach you to count all the way to 20!"

## 💡 Page 4: Learning 11–20

### 11–15

Read along with Alex:

- **11** — one ten + one one = **eleven**
- **12** — one ten + two ones = **twelve**
- **13** — one ten + three ones = **thirteen**
- **14** — one ten + four ones = **fourteen**
- **15** — one ten + five ones = **fifteen**

### 16–20

Keep counting!

| Number | Meaning | Say It |
|:------:|:--------|:-------|
| **16** | 10 + 6 | sixteen |
| **17** | 10 + 7 | seventeen |
| **18** | 10 + 8 | eighteen |
| **19** | 10 + 9 | nineteen |
| **20** | 10 + 10 = **two bundles!** | twenty |

<img src="./img/chapter-02/page-09.png" width="360" alt="Two bundles of 10 diamonds showing 20"/>

> **Tip:** In a two-digit number, the left digit tells you how many **tens**, and the right digit tells you how many **ones**.



## ❌ Common Mistakes

- ❌ **Thinking 12 is just "1 and 2"**
✅ **12 is "1 ten and 2 ones"** — not just a 1 and a 2 glued together!

- ❌ **Calling 20 just "two"**
✅ **20 is "2 tens"** — think of it as 2 bundles of 10 each.

- ❌ **Confusing teen numbers** (13 vs 30, 15 vs 50)
✅ Teen numbers have "teen" at the end (13-19) and always start with just 1 ten.



## 🧠 Think About It

1. **Observation**: If you have 1 bundle of 10 diamonds, and 4 extra diamonds, how many total? How do you know?

2. **What if**: In the number 15, if you take away 2 of the ones, does it still have 1 ten? What number do you get?



## 🔗 Cross-Curricular Links

- **Reading & Writing**: Read the number words: **eleven, twelve, thirteen… twenty**.
  Practice writing a full sentence: "15 is 1 ten and 5 ones."

- **Art**: Draw 15 blocks — color 10 of them red (one group) and the other 5 blue.
