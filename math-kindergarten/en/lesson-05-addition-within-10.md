# Lesson 5 — Addition Within 10

## 📋 Learning Goals
- Add numbers within 10
- Use a **number line** for addition
- Learn "**Make Ten**" strategy

---

> [Standard: K.OA.A.2 — Solve addition word problems within 10]
> [Standard: K.OA.A.4 — Find the number that makes 10 when added to any number from 1 to 9]

## 🎬 Page 1: Building a Bridge

A wide river blocked Steve and Alex's path.

> "We need to get to the other side to explore. But how?"

Alex looked around:

> "We have wooden planks. Let's build a bridge! You count the planks, I'll lay them."

Steve checked his backpack:

> "I have 4 planks…"

Alex looked at a pile on the ground:

> "I have 3 more. How many total? Is that enough to cross?"

## 🤔 Page 2: 4 + 3 = ?

Steve put the two piles together:

> "4 planks plus 3 planks… that's more than 5. I don't know how to add that."

Alex picked up a stick and drew a line on the ground:

> "Let me teach you the **number line**!"

She started from the left:

> "Start at **4**, jump forward **3** steps — 1, 2, 3. You land on **7**!"
> "So 4 + 3 = **7**."

<img src="./img/chapter-05/page-01.png" width="360" alt="Number line from 4 jumping 3 steps to 7"/>

## 👋 Page 3: Let's Try!

### 📏 Draw Your Own Number Line

Get a paper. Draw a line. Mark numbers 0 to 10.

> **Addition Secret**: Start at the bigger number, then jump forward!

**Try**: 3 + 5 = ?
Start at **5**, jump forward **3**: 6, 7, 8. Answer is **8**!

## 💎 Number Bonds: Make Ten

The most important Number Bond is **making 10**!

```
      10
     /  \
    3    7
```

> 3 and 7 make 10. If you know this, adding 7 to any number is easy!

**Try it**: 5 + 5 = ? &nbsp;&nbsp; 6 + 4 = ? &nbsp;&nbsp; 8 + 2 = ?

> All of these make 10! Practice your make-ten partners until they're automatic!

---

## ❌ Common Mistakes

- ❌ **Counting the starting number as a jump**
  Example: For 3 + 2, counting "3, 4, 5" and saying the answer is 6.
  ✅ **Correct**: Start **at** 3, jump forward 2: 4, 5. Answer is **5**.

- ❌ **Wrong make-ten partners**
  Example: Thinking 7 + 2 makes 10.
  ✅ **Correct**: Count from 7: 8, 9, 10. Need **3** more! Partner is 3.



## 🧠 Think About It

1. **Observation**: Look at 1+9=10, 2+8=10, 3+7=10…
  What pattern do you see? When one number gets bigger, what happens to the other?

2. **What if**: If you're on a number line at 4 and want to add 3, what if you jumped **left** instead of right? Would that still be addition? Why?



## 🔗 Cross-Curricular Links

- **Reading & Writing**: Say full sentences: "3 plus 2 equals 5."
  Look at a picture and describe: "There are 3 planks and 2 more planks — 5 planks total."

- **Physical Ed**: Hop forward the number of steps: start at step 1, hop 4 forward — you're at step 5!
