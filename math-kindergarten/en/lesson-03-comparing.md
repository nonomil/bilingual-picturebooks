## 👋 Page 3: Let's Try!

### 🧩 The One-to-One Method

Take two colors of blocks and line them up:
🟦🟦🟦🟦🟦
🟥🟥🟥

Line them up. Which color is longer? That one has **more**!

> **Secret to Comparing**: Line up → Compare → Find which has more

### 🔣 The Three Magic Symbols

Mathematicians use three symbols for comparing:

**`>`** — **Greater than** (big mouth eats the bigger number)
**`<`** — **Less than** (pointy end points to the smaller number)
**`=`** — **Equal** (both sides are the same)

> 💡 **Trick:** Think of the symbol as a hungry mouth. It always **opens toward the bigger number**!

<img src="./img/chapter-03/page-06.png" width="360" alt="Greater than, less than, and equal symbols"/>

**Try it:**
- **5 > 3** (5 is greater than 3, mouth faces 5)
- **2 < 7** (2 is less than 7, mouth faces 7)

## ❌ Common Mistakes

- ❌ **Judging by size, not count**
✅ When comparing **how many**, always count! A big object ≠ more objects.

- ❌ **Mixing up `>` and `<`**
✅ Remember: the hungry mouth **opens toward the BIGGER number**.
Example: `5 > 3`, mouth faces 5.



## 🧠 Think About It

1. **Observation**: Two groups of blocks look almost the same size. What's the best way to tell which has more? Count them? Match them one-to-one?

2. **What if**: There are 4 apples on the left and 2 more apples are added. Does the comparison symbol change? Why?
