# Lesson 13: Action Time! 🏃

## 📋 Learning Goals
- Action words: **run, walk, jump, sit, stand, dance, swim**
- Sight words: **came, did, ran, saw, say**
- Sentence: "I can ___." / "Look at me ___."

## Page 2: Run and Walk 🏃🚶

> "First game — The Relay Race! You must **run**!"

```
   R-U-N → run 🏃
```

> "Run means to move very fast on your feet."

```
   🏃 Run:
   - move fast on your feet
   - faster than walking
   - heart beats fast! ❤️💨
   
   I can run fast!
   He is RUNNING in the race!
```

Steve gets ready at the starting line. "On your marks... get set... GO!"

Vroom! Steve runs like the wind. His feet fly across the grass.

After the race, the teacher says: "Now we cool down. Let's **walk** slowly."

```
   W-A-L-K → walk 🚶
```

> "Walk means to move on your feet, but slowly."

```
   🚶 Walk:
   - move on your feet
   - slower than running
   - one step at a time
   
   Let's walk to the park.
   I am WALKING with my friend.
```

**Sight word: ran (run的过去式)**
> "Steve **ran** very fast in the race!"

```
   Today: I run.
   Yesterday: I ran.
```

## Page 3: Jump and Sit 🦘🪑

> "Next game — Sack Race! You must **jump**!"

```
   J-U-M-P → jump 🦘
```

> "Jump means to push off the ground with both feet."

```
   🦘 Jump:
   - push off the ground
   - both feet leave the ground
   - you go UP!
   
   I can jump high!
   The frog is JUMPING.
```

Everyone gets into big sacks. Hop, hop, hop! Alex jumps so high she almost flies!

> "Jump! Jump! Jump!" everyone cheers.

After jumping, they all feel tired.

> "Let's **sit** down and rest," says the teacher.

```
   S-I-T → sit 🪑
```

> "Sit means to put your bottom on a chair or the ground."

```
   🪑 Sit:
   - put your body on a chair
   - rest your legs
   - opposite of stand
   
   Please sit down.
   I am SITTING on the grass.
```

**Sight word: came (come的过去式)**
> "We **came** to Sports Day early! Now we are tired!"


After Sports Day, the teacher takes everyone to the village pool.

> "Now the best part — **swim** time!"

```
   S-W-I-M → swim 🏊
```

```

   - use arms and legs
```

The water sparkles in the sun. What a perfect end to Sports Day!

## Page 6: Action Freeze Game 🎮

Back on the field, the teacher has one more game.

> "Let's play Action Freeze!"

```
   🎮 Action Freeze Game
   
   When I say an action — you DO it!
   When I say FREEZE ❄️ — you STOP!
```

The teacher calls out:

> "**RUN!**" — Everyone runs.
> "**FREEZE!**" — Everyone stops like statues!
> "**JUMP!**" — Everyone jumps.
> "**FREEZE!**" — Mid-air freeze!
> "**DANCE!**" — Everyone dances.
> "**FREEZE!**" — Funny dance poses!
> "**SWIM!**" — Everyone swims on the grass.
> "**FREEZE!**" — Fish out of water!
> "**WALK!**" — Everyone walks slowly.
> "**FREEZE!**" — Mid-step!

Everyone collapses in laughter. "That was the best game ever!"

```
   🎮 Action Review:
   
   🏃 Run — fast!
   🚶 Walk — slow!
   🦘 Jump — up!
   🪑 Sit — down!
   🧍 Stand — on feet!
   💃 Dance — to music!
```

## Page 7: Story Time — The Race to the Big Tree 🌳

One sunny morning, Steve and Alex decide to race to the Big Tree at the top of the hill.

> "Ready, set, GO!"

They **run** up the hill. Steve is fast, but Alex runs faster!

Halfway up, they get tired. "Let's **walk** for a bit," pants Steve.

They **walk** slowly, enjoying the flowers and butterflies.

Then Alex has an idea. "Let's **jump** over every rock we see!"

Hop, hop, hop! They **jump** over ten rocks.

At the top, they **sit** under the Big Tree. "We made it!"

After resting, they **stand** up to look at the view. The whole village is below them.

> "This is beautiful!" says Alex. She starts to **dance** with joy.

Back at the village lake, they cool off with a **swim**. Splash, kick, float!

> "What an active day!" says Steve. "We ran, walked, jumped, sat, stood, danced, and swam!"

> "Seven actions in one day!" Alex counts. "Tomorrow, let's do it all over again!"

And they did.

### ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|----------|---------|
| "She go to school" | **"She goes to school"** — he/she/it + verb+s |
| "I'm go to the village" | **"I'm going to the village"** — am/is/are + verb-ing |
| "He is in the bus" | **"He is on the bus"** — on for vehicles, in for buildings |
| "I feeled happy" | **"I felt happy"** — irregular past: feel→felt |

### 🧠 Think About It
1. **Pattern**: "I walk → I walked" but "I go → I went, I see → I saw". Why are some past tense words so different?
2. **What if**: If Steve could only use 10 English words to survive in the Minecraft village, which 10 would he pick?
