## Page 2: Ball and Doll ⚽🪆

Steve goes first. He holds up a round black-and-white object.

> "**This** is my **ball**!"

```
   B-A-L-L → ball ⚽
```

> "I play with my ball every day. I can kick it, throw it, and catch it!"

```

   - fun to play with friends
```

Next is Alex. She holds up a small figure with yarn hair.

> "**This** is my **doll**!"

```
   D-O-L-L → doll 🪆
```

> "My grandma made this doll for me. I love her!"

**Sight word: me**
> "My grandma made this doll for **me**."

**me** = 我 — myself (after the verb)

```
   This is for me.
   Look at me!
   Come with me!
```

## Page 5: Jump Rope Fun 🪢

After Show and Tell, the teacher brings out something fun.

> "Who wants to **jump**?"

```
   J-U-M-P → jump 🦘

```

> "I can **jump**! Watch me!"

**jump** = 跳 — push off the ground with your feet

## Page 8: Story Time — The Lost Pencil ✏️

After school, Alex cannot find her yellow pencil.

> "My pencil! Where is my pencil?"

She looks in her bag. Not there.
She looks on her desk. Not there.
She looks under the table. Not there!

> "Oh no! My favorite pencil!"

Steve comes to help. "Let me think... Where did you use it last?"

```
   🤔 Think, think, think...
   
   Where was the pencil?
```

> "I drew a flower on the board!"

They look by the board. There it is! The little yellow pencil, right by the chalk.

> "I found it!" Alex picks it up happily.

> "Thank you, Steve! You helped me find my pencil."

> "That is what friends do," Steve says.

She puts the pencil safely in her bag. "I will never lose you again, little pencil!"

```
   ✏️ Pencil's day:
   
   📖 in the bag
   ✏️ draw on paper
   📋 left by the board
   🎒 back in the bag — safe!
```

### ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|----------|---------|
| "I like apple" (singular) | **"I like apples"** — when talking about things in general, use plural |
| "It's rain" | **"It's raining"** — use -ing for actions happening now |
| "I have 3 foot" | **"I have 3 feet"** — irregular plural: foot→feet |
| "The dog big" | **"The dog is big"** — need "is" (be verb) |

### 🧠 Think About It
1. **Pattern**: "Cat→cats, dog→dogs" but "mouse→mice, child→children". Why do some words change differently?
2. **What if**: What if English weather words were all Minecraft-style? Instead of "sunny", you'd say "torch-bright". Can you create 3 more?
