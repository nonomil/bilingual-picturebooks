# Lesson 9 Extension: Let's Make a Picnic! 🧺

## 📋 Learning Goals
- Review food words: apple, banana, milk, bread, egg, cake, water
- Review sight words: eat, get, like, want, good
- New words: **juice, rice, soup, cookie, picnic**
- Sentence: "Let's make ___." / "I have ___."
- 🔤 Sound Block review: ch says /tʃ/ + tch words

---

## 🔤 Sound Block Review: ch and tch = /tʃ/!

```
   🎵 NOTE BLOCK GLOWING!

   Review: /tʃ/ — two ways to write it!

   ch says /tʃ/:  chip 🍟  chin 🧔  chest 📦  chop 🪓  lunch 🍱
   
   tch says /tʃ/ too! (at the end of short words)
   c-a-tch → catch! 🤾
   m-a-tch → match! 🔥
   p-a-tch → patch! 🩹
   w-a-tch → watch! ⌚
   f-e-tch → fetch! 🎾

   /tʃ/ /tʃ/ /tʃ/ — ch and tch, same sound!
```

## Page 1: A Picnic Idea 💡

The next morning, the sun is shining bright.

> "What a beautiful day!" says Alex.

Steve looks out the window. "Let's go on a **picnic**!"

```
   P-I-C-N-I-C → picnic 🧺
   
   picnic = 野餐 — eating food outside!
```

> "Great idea! We need food for the picnic."

Alex makes a list:

```
   🧺 PICNIC LIST 🧺
   
   □ bread 🍞
   □ apples 🍎
   □ bananas 🍌
   □ water 💧
   □ something new...
```

> "Let's go to the village shop and GET more food!"

**Sight word practice:**
```
   I WANT to have a picnic. 🧺
   Let's GET food from the shop. 🏪
   I LIKE picnics! 🌳
   We can EAT outside! ☀️
   GOOD food for a GOOD day! 😊
```

## Page 2: Juice and Rice 🧃🍚

At the village shop, the shopkeeper welcomes them.

> "What do you need for your picnic?"

Alex looks at the shelves. "We have water. But do you have **juice**?"

```
   J-U-I-C-E → juice 🧃
```

The shopkeeper hands her a bottle of orange juice. "Fresh orange juice!"

> "Juice is sweet and cold. Perfect for a picnic!"

```
   🧃 Juice:
   - made from fruit
   - sweet and cold
   - orange juice, apple juice, grape juice
```

Steve spots a big bag. "What is that?"

> "**Rice**!" says the shopkeeper. "We can make rice balls for your picnic."

```
   R-I-C-E → rice 🍚
```

> "Rice is white and soft. Many people eat rice every day!"

The shopkeeper shows them how to make rice balls — little round balls of rice. "Put a little salt on top. Yummy!"

```
   🍚 Rice:
   - white, small grains
   - soft when cooked
   - many people eat rice every day
```

## Page 3: Soup and Cookie 🍜🍪

> "What about something warm?" asks Alex.

The shopkeeper points to a big pot on the stove. "**Soup**!"

```
   S-O-U-P → soup 🍲
```

> "Soup is warm and good. It has vegetables and water."

He gives them each a small taste. "Mmm! Vegetable soup!"

```
   🍲 Soup:
   - warm and comforting
   - made with water and vegetables
   - chicken soup, tomato soup, vegetable soup
```

Then, Alex sees a jar on the counter. Little round brown things.

> "Are those **cookies**?"

```
   C-O-O-K-I-E → cookie 🍪
```

> "Yes! Fresh baked cookies. Take some for your picnic!"

Steve's eyes go wide. "Cookies! I LOVE cookies!"

The shopkeeper wraps up six cookies. "One for each of you — and some for friends!"

**🔤 Sound Block practice:**
```
   tch says /tʃ/!
   
   c-a-tch → catch! 🤾
   Catch the cookie! 🍪
   
   m-a-tch → match! 🔥
   Cookies and milk — a perfect match!
```

## Page 5: The Perfect Spot 🌳

They walk outside the village to find a picnic spot.

> "Where should we sit?" asks Alex.

Steve points. "Look! Under that big tree!"

A giant oak tree stands on a green hill. Under it, the grass is soft. Flowers bloom all around. You can see the whole village from here.

> "The perfect picnic spot!"

They spread a blanket under the tree.

```
   🌳 Perfect Picnic Spot:
   
   ☀️ warm sun
   🌿 soft grass
   🌸 pretty flowers
   🏘️ view of the village
   🌬️ gentle wind
```

Alex arranges all the food on the blanket. "It looks like a feast!"

Steve pours juice for both of them. "Cheers! To picnics!"

> "Cheers! 🧃🧃"

## Page 6: Picnic Guests 🐿️

As they eat, they hear a rustling sound.

A little squirrel peeks out from behind the tree!

> "Chip chip!"

> "A squirrel!" says Alex. "I think he wants food."

Steve breaks off a tiny piece of bread. "Here, little one."

The squirrel hops closer, takes the bread, and nibbles it. So cute!

Then... another visitor. Two bluebirds land on a branch above them.

> "Tweet! Tweet! Cookies?"

Alex laughs. "I think everyone wants our picnic!"

Soon they have a little picnic party:

```
   🧺 Picnic Party 🧺
   
   👦 Steve     — eating a rice ball 🍙
   👧 Alex      — drinking juice 🧃
   🐿️ Squirrel  — nibbling bread 🍞
   🐦 Bluebird  — pecking cookie crumbs 🍪
   🐦 Bluebird  — singing a song 🎵
```

> "We should have a picnic every week!" says Steve.

**Sight word practice:**
```
   I LIKE picnics with friends!
   The animals WANT food too.
   This is a GOOD day.
   Let's EAT together!
```

## Page 7: Story Time — The Sandwich Adventure 🥪

After the picnic, the shopkeeper waves them over.

> "You know, there is one more food you should try."

> "A **sandwich**! Bread with things inside!"

```
   S-A-N-D-W-I-C-H → sandwich 🥪
```

The shopkeeper shows them. "You take two pieces of bread, and put anything inside!"

Steve gets an idea. "Let's make our OWN sandwiches!"

```
   🥪 DIY Sandwich:
   
   Bread 🍞
   + Egg 🥚
   + Tomato
   + Lettuce
   
   = YUMMY SANDWICH! 🥪
```

Each person makes their own:

```
   Steve's sandwich:  🍞 + 🥚 + 🍎 slices
   Alex's sandwich:   🍞 + 🥚 + 🍌 slices
   Shopkeeper:        🍞 + 🥚 + soup dip
```

> "Mine is apple-egg!" says Steve.

> "Mine is banana-egg!" says Alex.

They taste each other's sandwiches. "Both are GOOD!"

> "That is the best thing about food," the shopkeeper says. "You can make it your own way!"

**🔤 Sound Block practice:**
```
   The word "sandwich" has a secret!
   s-a-n-d-w-i-c-h
   
   ch at the end says /tʃ/!
   sand-wich → sandwich 🥪
   
   lunch → /tʃ/
   sandwich → /tʃ/
   munch → /tʃ/
```
