## Page 1: How to Tell a Story 🎭

The librarian teaches the class about storytelling.

> "Every story has three parts: a **beginning**, a middle, and an **end**."

```
   📖 Story Structure:
   
   BEGINNING: "Once upon a time..."
   → Where? Who? What do they want?
   
   MIDDLE: The adventure!
   → What happens? What goes wrong?
   
   END: "And they all lived happily!"
   → How does it end? What was learned?
```

```
   O-N-C-E → once 🕰️
   S-T-O-R-Y → story 📖
   B-E-G-I-N-N-I-N-G → beginning 🏁
   E-N-D → end 🏁
   A-D-V-E-N-T-U-R-E → adventure 🗺️
```

> "Once upon a time, a brave boy and a smart girl went on an **adventure**..."

> "That's how all great stories begin!" says the teacher.

## Page 2: Tell Your Own Story ✏️

> "Now it's your turn. Write your own short story!"

Steve writes:

```
   📖 The Magic Apple
   
   Once upon a time, I found a magic apple.
   It was GOLD and it could talk!
   The apple said, "Make a WISH!"
   I wished for all my friends to be HAPPY.
   And they were. The end.
```

Alex writes:

```
   📖 The Lost Star
   
   Once upon a time, a star fell from the sky.
   It landed in the PARK.
   I READ a map and FOUND the star.
   I put it back in the SKY.
   Now it shines every NIGHT. The end.
```

> "See? You already know enough words to tell wonderful stories!"

### ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|----------|---------|
| "He don't like it" | **"He doesn't like it"** — he/she/it uses doesn't |
| "There is many blocks" | **"There are many blocks"** — many = plural = are |
| "Can you help me?" "Yes, I help" | **"Yes, I can"** or **"Yes, I will help you"** |
| Reading word by word, not in phrases | Read in **meaning chunks**: "Steve walked / into the cave / and saw a light" |

### 🧠 Think About It
1. **Story**: In "The Lost Cat", why does Alex feel sad? What clues in the story tell you about her feelings?
2. **What if**: What if the treasure map in the last story was written in a language nobody could read? How would Steve and Alex solve the problem?
