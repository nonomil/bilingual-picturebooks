## Page 1: Ginger Is Gone!

It **was** a sunny morning. Steve **went** to feed his cat, Ginger.

But Ginger **was** not in her bed. She **was** not in the kitchen. She **was** not in the garden.

> "Ginger is **lost**!" cried Steve.

```
   L-O-S-T → lost 😿
   
   lost = 丢了，不见了 — cannot find
   
   "I lost my pencil. I lost my cat!"
```

Steve felt sad. His orange cat was nowhere to be seen.

> "**Where** can she be?" he wondered.

> "**When** did you last see her?" asked Alex, running over.

> "Last night! She **was** sleeping on my bed!"

<img src="./img/chapter-19-en/page-02.png" width="480" alt="Minecraft Steve looking at empty cat bed, empty food bowl, worried face, Alex running to help"/>

---

## Page 2: We Must Find Her

> "Don't worry," said Alex. "We will **find** her!"

```
   F-I-N-D → find 🔍
   
   find = 找到 — discover, get back
```

> "Let's **look** everywhere!"

```
   L-O-O-K → look 👀
   
   look = 看、寻找 — search with your eyes
```

They **looked** under the bed. No Ginger.
They **looked** behind the sofa. No Ginger.
They **looked** in the closet. No Ginger!

> "We need **help**," said Steve.

```
   H-E-L-P → help 🤝
   
   help = 帮助 — assist, give support
```

Alex called their **friends**. Tom, Lily, and Mia came running.

> "**What** happened?" asked Mia.

> "My cat is **lost**!" said Steve. "**Help** us **find** her!"

The search team was ready.

<img src="./img/chapter-19-en/page-03.png" width="480" alt="Minecraft Steve and Alex searching under bed behind sofa in closet, friends Tom Lily Mia running to help, search team"/>

## Page 3: The Search

The friends split up to **look** for Ginger.

Tom **went** to the garden. "Ginger! Ginger!" No cat.

Lily **went** to the park. "Ginger! Ginger!" No cat.

Mia **went** to the shop. "Have you seen an orange cat?" No one had.

Steve and Alex **went** to the farm. "Ginger! Ginger!"

Just then, they heard a tiny sound. "Meow..."

> "**What** was that?" Alex stopped.

> "**Where** is it coming from?" Steve listened carefully.

"Meow... meow..."

It was coming from the big oak tree! High up in the branches, a little orange face peeked out.

> "Ginger! We **found** you!" cried Steve.

```
   F-O-U-N-D → found ✅ (find 的过去式)
   
   I lost... → I looked... → I FOUND!
```

<img src="./img/chapter-19-en/page-04.png" width="480" alt="Minecraft friends searching garden park shop farm, Steve and Alex at big oak tree, Ginger's face in branches, found moment"/>

---

## Page 4: Coming Home

Ginger was stuck high in the tree. She was scared.

> "How do we get her down?" asked Alex.

Tom had an idea. He ran **home** and got a ladder.

```
   H-O-M-E → home 🏠
   
   home = 家 — the place you live, your safe place
```

Tom climbed the ladder. "Come here, Ginger. It's okay."

Carefully, he reached out and scooped up the orange cat. Ginger purred and rubbed against his hand.

> "You **found** her!" cheered Mia.

> "Thank you, **friend**," said Steve to Tom.

```
   F-R-I-E-N-D → friend 👫
   
   friend = 朋友 — someone you like and trust
```

Steve held Ginger close. "Don't run away again, little friend."

Ginger purred and licked Steve's nose. She was **home**, safe and sound.

<img src="./img/chapter-19-en/page-05.png" width="480" alt="Minecraft Ginger stuck in tree, Tom with ladder climbing up, rescuing Ginger, Steve holding Ginger, friends celebrating"/>

## Page 5: Story Complete!

That night, Steve sat with Ginger on his lap.

> "Today I **was** so scared," said Steve. "When I **saw** Ginger was **lost**, I didn't know **what** to do."

> "But your friends **helped** you," said Alex. "And we **found** her together."

Steve nodded. "Friends are the best. When I **was** sad, they **helped** me. When Ginger **was** lost, they **looked** and **looked**. And now she is home!"

Ginger purred happily. She had a big adventure — but the best part was coming **home** to her **friend**.

### ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|----------|---------|
| "He don't like it" | **"He doesn't like it"** — he/she/it uses doesn't |
| "There is many blocks" | **"There are many blocks"** — many = plural = are |
| "Can you help me?" "Yes, I help" | **"Yes, I can"** or **"Yes, I will help you"** |
| Reading word by word, not in phrases | Read in **meaning chunks**: "Steve walked / into the cave / and saw a light" |
