## Page 1: Look Outside! 🌤️

Steve wakes up and stretches. He looks out the window.

> "What is the weather today?"

```
   weather = 天气
   
   Every day the weather can be different:
   ☀️ sunny     🌧️ rainy     ☁️ cloudy
   💨 windy     ❄️ snowy     🔥 hot     🥶 cold
```

Alex knocks on the door. "Steve! Look at the sky!"

Steve runs outside. The sun is shining bright!

> "It is a **sun**-ny day!"

```
   S-U-N → sun ☀️
   
   The sun is big and bright.
   The sun makes us warm.
   The sun is a star!
```

**Sight word: do**
> "**Do** you see the sun?"

**do** = (question helper) — used to ask questions

```
   Do you like the sun? ☀️
   Do you see the bird? 🐦
   Do you want to play? ⚽
```

## Page 2: Rain 🌧️

But wait — a dark cloud rolls in. The sun hides.

Plip. Plop. Plip-plop!

> "**Rain**!" says Alex.

```
   R-A-I-N → rain 🌧️
```

> "Rain falls from the clouds. It waters the plants and trees."

Steve puts out his hand. Little drops fall on his palm. "The rain is cold!"

```
   🌧️ Rain:
   - falls from clouds
   - waters plants 🌱
   - makes puddles! 💦
   
   We say: "It is raining."
```

**Sight word: under**
> "Come **under** the tree! We can stay dry."

**under** = 下面 — below, not on top

```
   Under the tree. 🌳
   Under the table. 🪑
   Under the umbrella. ☂️
```

They run under a big oak tree. The rain patters on the leaves above them.

## Page 3: Cloud and Wind ☁️💨

Looking up at the sky, Alex points.

> "So many **clouds**!"

```
   C-L-O-U-D → cloud ☁️
```

> "Clouds are white and fluffy. They float in the sky."

```
   ☁️ Clouds:
   - white, grey, or dark
   - made of tiny water drops
   - float high in the sky
   
   White clouds = good weather
   Dark clouds = rain is coming!
```

Suddenly, the trees start to sway. Whoosh!

> "**Wind**!" says Steve. "The wind is blowing the clouds!"

```
   W-I-N-D → wind 💨
```

> "Wind is moving air. You cannot see it, but you can feel it!"

```
   💨 Wind:
   - moving air
   - can be soft or strong
   - makes trees dance 🌳💃
   
   Soft wind = breeze 🍃
   Strong wind = storm ⛈️
```

The wind blows the dark cloud away. The sun peeks out again!

## Page 4: Snow ❄️

As they walk further from the village, the air gets colder.

Steve's breath makes little white puffs.

> "It is so cold up here!"

They reach a hilltop — and everything is white!

> "**Snow**!" Alex shouts with joy.

```
   S-N-O-W → snow ❄️
```

> "Snow is frozen water. It falls from the sky in little white flakes."

```
   ❄️ Snow:
   - frozen water
   - white and cold
   - falls in winter
   - you can make snowmen! ⛄
```

Steve catches a snowflake on his tongue. "Cold and tickly!"

They roll big balls of snow and make a snowman. Two stone eyes, a carrot nose, stick arms.

> "Hello, Mr. Snowman!" ☃️

**Sight word: soon**
> "**Soon** the snow will melt. Let's play now!"

**soon** = 很快 — in a short time, not long

```
   Soon the sun will come out.
   Soon we will go home.
   Play soon!
```

## Page 5: Hot and Cold 🔥🥶

Back in the village, the weather changes again. The sun is strong now.

> "I am **hot**!" Steve fans himself.

```
   H-O-T → hot 🔥
```

> "Hot is when the temperature is high. You feel warm — too warm!"

```
   🔥 Hot:
   - high temperature
   - the sun makes us hot
   - you sweat 💦
   - drink cold water! 💧
```

They sit in the shade. But then the sun goes behind a mountain. The temperature drops.

> "Now I am **cold**!" Alex shivers.

```
   C-O-L-D → cold 🥶
```

> "Cold is when the temperature is low. You feel chilly!"

```
   🥶 Cold:
   - low temperature
   - you shiver 🥶
   - wear a coat! 🧥
   - snow is cold ❄️
```

**Sight word: did**
> "**Did** you feel that? First hot, now cold!"

**did** = 做了（do 的过去式）

```
   Did you see the sun?
   Did you feel the wind?
   Did you play in the snow?
```

## Page 6: Weather Report 📺

Steve has an idea. "Let's be weather reporters!"

He picks up a stick as his microphone. Alex draws a weather map in the sand.

```
   🎤 WEATHER REPORT 🎤
   
   "Good morning, village!
    Here is your weather:
   
   ☀️ Today: SUNNY and HOT
    The sun will shine all day!
   
   ☁️ Afternoon: some CLOUDS
    But no rain.
   
   🌧️ Evening: RAIN is coming
    Stay dry under a tree!
   
   ❄️ Mountain: SNOW on top
    Wear a coat. It is COLD!
   
   💨 WIND will blow from the west.
   
   That is all for today.
   Back to you, Alex!"
```

Alex takes the stick. "Thank you, Steve! What a day of weather!"

Villagers passing by stop to watch and laugh. "Best weather report ever!"

**Sight word: that**
> "**That** was a good report! Do it again!"

**that** = 那个 — the thing over there (not this)

```
   That cloud is dark. ☁️
   I like that snowman. ⛄
   That was fun!
```

## Page 7: Story Time — The Cloud That Could Not Rain ☁️

High in the sky, there was a little cloud named Puffy.

Puffy watched the other clouds rain. But Puffy could not rain!

> "Why can't I rain?" Puffy was sad.

The Sun came to help. "You need more water, little cloud! Float over the big lake."

Puffy floated over the lake and drank up water. BIGGER and BIGGER!

The Wind came to help. "You need to be cold, little cloud! Let me blow you to the mountain."

Puffy floated to the cold mountain. Colder and COLDER!

Now Puffy was big, cold, and full of water.

> "I think... I think I can rain!" Plip! Plop! PLIP-PLOP!

Puffy rained on the thirsty flowers. The flowers turned bright colors and bloomed!

> "I DID it!" Puffy cheered. "I rained!"

All the other clouds gathered around. "Good job, Puffy!"

From that day on, Puffy was the happiest rain cloud in the sky. When you see a little white cloud, wave hello — it might be Puffy, getting ready to rain! 🌧️☁️

### ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|----------|---------|
| "I like apple" (singular) | **"I like apples"** — when talking about things in general, use plural |
| "It's rain" | **"It's raining"** — use -ing for actions happening now |
| "I have 3 foot" | **"I have 3 feet"** — irregular plural: foot→feet |
| "The dog big" | **"The dog is big"** — need "is" (be verb) |

### 🧠 Think About It
1. **Pattern**: "Cat→cats, dog→dogs" but "mouse→mice, child→children". Why do some words change differently?
2. **What if**: What if English weather words were all Minecraft-style? Instead of "sunny", you'd say "torch-bright". Can you create 3 more?
