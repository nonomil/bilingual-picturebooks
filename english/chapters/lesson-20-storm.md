## Page 1: Dark Clouds

It **was** a quiet afternoon. Steve and Alex **were** playing in the park.

Suddenly, the sky turned **dark**.

```
   D-A-R-K → dark 🌑
   
   dark = 黑、暗 — no light
```

> "Look at the **clouds**!" said Alex. "They are getting darker and **darker**!"

```
   C-L-O-U-D → cloud ☁️
```

Big **wind** started to blow. The trees swayed. Leaves flew everywhere.

```
   W-I-N-D → wind 💨
```

> "A **storm** is coming!" cried Steve.

```
   S-T-O-R-M → storm 🌩️
   
   storm = 暴风雨 — very bad weather with wind, rain, thunder
```

**Sight word: our**
> "**Our** picnic! We must take **our** things **home**!"

**our** = 我们的 — belonging to us

```
   This is our house. 🏠
   Our friends are here. 👫
```

## Page 2: Rain and Thunder

The first drops of **rain** fell. Then more. And more!

```
   R-A-I-N → rain 🌧️
```

> "Run!" shouted Steve. "We **have** to find shelter!"

```
   H-A-V-E → have 🏠
   
   We have to go! (must)
   I have an umbrella. ☂️
   They have a plan.
```

CRACK! BOOM! Thunder roared across the sky. Lightning flashed.

> "I'm scared!" said Alex, covering her ears.

> "Me **too**!" said Steve. "Let's get to a **safe** place!"

```
   S-A-F-E → safe 🛡️
   
   safe = 安全 — not in danger
```

**Sight word: too**
> "I want to be safe **too**!"

**too** = 也 — also

```
   I like it too. 😊
   Me too! ✋
   Too many clouds! ☁️☁️☁️
```

They spotted a small hut. "**There**! Let's go **there**!"

## Page 3: Safe in the Hut

They made it **into** the hut just as the rain got heavier.

Inside, it was warm and dry. An old **farmer** lived there.

> "**Come** in, **come** in!" said the farmer. "You are **safe** **here**!"

```
   C-O-M-E → come 🚶
   
   come = 来 — move toward
   
   Come here! 👋
   Come in! 🚪
```

**Sight word: here**
> "You can stay **here** until the storm passes."

**here** = 这里 — in this place

```
   Come here! 👋
   I am here. 📍
   Put it here.
```

The farmer gave them warm blankets and hot tea.

> "Thank you," said Alex. "We were so scared."

> "Storms **come** and go," said the farmer. "The important thing is to find a **safe** place. And you found one **here**."

## Page 4: After the Storm

The storm raged for an hour. Then, slowly, the **rain** stopped. The **wind** calmed. The sky got brighter.

> "The storm is over!" said Alex, looking out the window.

They thanked the farmer and stepped outside. The world looked fresh and clean.

```
   After the storm:
   🌧️ rain → stopped
   💨 wind → calmed
   ☁️ dark clouds → gone
   ☀️ sun → coming out!
```

> "Everything smells so fresh!" said Steve.

> "And look!" Alex pointed. A beautiful rainbow arched across the sky. 🌈

**Sight word: well**
> "All is **well** now," said the farmer with a smile.

**well** = 好 — good, healthy, fine

```
   I am well. 😊
   All is well. ✅
   Well done! 👏
```

> "**Our** adventure today **was** scary," said Alex. "But we **came** through it together."

> "And now we have a new friend," said Steve, waving to the farmer.

## Page 5: Story Complete!

That night, back **home**, Steve and Alex talked about the storm.

> "I **was** so scared when the thunder came," said Alex.

> "Me **too**," said Steve. "But we found a **safe** place. And we helped each other."

> "That's what friends do," said Alex. "In the **dark** and in the **storm**, we stick together."

### ⚠️ Common Mistakes

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|----------|---------|
| "The storm was more stronger" | **"The storm was stronger"** — stronger already means "more strong", no double comparative |
| "I am scare of thunder" | **"I am scared of thunder"** — scare is a verb, scared is the adjective |
| "The wind blowed the trees" | **"The wind blew the trees"** — blow→blew is irregular |
| "We goed to the hut for safety" | **"We went to the hut for safety"** — go→went is irregular |

### 🧠 Think About It
1. **Feelings**: Alex was scared during the storm. What helped her feel better? What do you do when you feel scared or worried?
2. **Science**: Lightning flashes first, then thunder comes later. Why? What could you count to know how far away the storm is?
